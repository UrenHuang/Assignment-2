Global COVID-19 vaccination status

In this data visualization, maps are used to show our world's COVID-19 vaccination status, including the distribution of the total number of people in the world who have been vaccinated. Bubble plots are used to show the number of people recovering from COVID-19 in different continents, as well as the proportion of different brands of vaccines used in Europe and the proportion of different brands of vaccines in several major countries in the world over time.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total number of people who received at least one vaccine dose around the world

The vaccine data is presented on a map with labels on the right. And we can see more visually that the darker blue areas are where the most people have registered for the vaccine. (Areas without data are marked in greay).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The number of people on different continents who have recovered from COVID-19

We can see the number of people who have recovered from COVID-19 in different continents, as well as the number of people who have recovered from COVID-19 in the corresponding country by selecting the name of a G7 country from the selection bar.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Explore the use of different brands of vaccines in Europe

The chart shows the proportion of different brands in some countries. We found that Pfizer accounts for far more than any other brand, particularly in the European Union. Pfizer and Moderna have a similar share of vaccine use in the U.S., and a small amount of J&J vaccine is used in the U.S.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Explore the use of different brands of vaccines in some countries

The chart shows the proportion of different brands of vaccine used in Europe over time. It can be found that from the end of 2020 to October 2021, Pfizer vaccines accounted for the largest proportion in Europe, followed by Oxford/AstraZeneca vaccines and Sinopharm/Beijing and Sputnik V vaccines.
